Payment gateway

Integration involves setting up the payment gateway software and configuring it to work with the e-commerce website or other platform. The payment gateway serves as a mediator between the merchant's website and the payment processor, ensuring that the payment is processed securely and that customer data is kept confidential. Payment gateway integration also enables merchants to offer a wider range of payment options to their customers, such as credit cards, debit cards, and e-wallets.

High security standards

High security is essential for any payment system to protect sensitive user data and prevent fraud. Multiple layers of security, such as encryption and tokenization ensure that data is protected at all times. Security measures are regularly audited and updated to keep up with new threats and vulnerabilities.

Payment routing and cascading

Two strategies used in payment processing to optimize transaction success rates and reduce processing costs

Payment routing involves selecting the most optimal payment processor to handle a transaction based on factors such as transaction amount, currency, and location. By routing transactions to the most appropriate processor, merchants can increase the likelihood of transaction success and reduce the risk of declined transactions. Cascading is a technique used when processors are available. It involves sending a transaction to the primary processor, and if that processor declines the transaction, routing it to the next available processor until the transaction is successfully processed. Cascading helps to increase transaction success rates and reduce the risk of lost sales.
